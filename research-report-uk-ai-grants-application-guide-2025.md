# UK AI Grants: Application Guide for Very Small Companies

## Executive Summary

Very small UK companies (micro businesses and early-stage SMEs) can access meaningful AI support, but success depends on targeting the right programme and submitting a practical, evidence-based application. Most grants are competitive and designed to fund **innovation**, not routine IT upgrades. The strongest applications show a clear business problem, measurable outcomes, credible delivery plan, and an appropriate budget.

This guide explains where to look, what assessors expect, and how small teams can prepare a high-quality grant submission without large bid-writing resources.

## Who This Guide Is For

This report is intended for:

- UK companies with fewer than 50 staff (especially fewer than 10)
- Founders, operations leads, and product teams planning AI adoption
- Businesses applying for innovation or growth funding in 2025
- Teams needing a practical structure for first-time applications

## UK Funding Landscape for AI (2025)

The most relevant opportunities usually come from a mix of national, regional, and sector channels:

1. **Innovate UK competitions** (often through UKRI)
   - Strong fit for novel AI-enabled products, services, or processes
   - Usually requires technical merit plus commercial impact

2. **Regional growth and business support programmes**
   - Combined authorities, devolved administrations, and local innovation initiatives
   - Sometimes lower-value but easier entry points for micro firms

3. **University and Catapult collaborations**
   - Access to specialist facilities and technical partners
   - Useful where internal AI capability is still developing

4. **Sector-specific digital transformation calls**
   - Common in manufacturing, health, energy, and creative industries
   - Assessors expect domain relevance, not generic AI claims

## What Funders Typically Want

Across programmes, assessors consistently score four things:

### 1) Problem Clarity

You must show a specific pain point (cost, delays, quality, risk, missed revenue) and why AI is an appropriate method.

### 2) Innovation and Additionality

Funders look for work that would not happen at the same pace or scale without support. Replacing standard software spend with grant funding is usually weak.

### 3) Delivery Confidence

A credible plan matters more than ambition alone. Assessors want to see milestones, accountable owners, realistic timeframes, and delivery dependencies.

### 4) Impact and Value for Money

You should quantify likely outcomes (productivity, margin, quality, emissions, jobs, export potential). Estimates can be conservative but should be evidence-led.

## Practical Eligibility Checks Before You Start

Before spending time on writing, confirm:

- Company registration and UK operating footprint meet rules
- Company size classification matches call requirements
- Project start/end dates align with competition window
- Match funding requirements are affordable from cash flow
- Excluded costs (e.g., routine operating costs) are not central to budget
- Collaboration requirements (if any) can be met in time

A quick early compliance review can save weeks of effort.

## Building a Strong Application Narrative

A simple structure works well for very small companies:

1. **Current state**: What is happening now and why it is a business constraint
2. **Proposed AI intervention**: What will be built or implemented
3. **Evidence base**: Why this approach should work (data, pilots, benchmarks)
4. **Execution plan**: Work packages, milestones, governance, risk control
5. **Outcomes**: Quantified commercial and operational impact
6. **Scalability**: How benefits continue beyond grant period

Avoid broad statements like “we will use AI to improve efficiency.” Replace with measurable claims linked to a baseline.

## Budget Design for Micro Teams

For small companies, budget quality can be the deciding factor. Good practice includes:

- Mapping each cost line to a deliverable and milestone
- Separating internal labour, subcontracting, software, and equipment clearly
- Justifying external consultants by specialist need and time-limited scope
- Including test/validation spend rather than only development spend
- Showing contingency logic through risk mitigation, not padded totals

Where possible, include assumptions behind labour day rates and effort estimates. Transparency builds assessor confidence.

## Common Weaknesses in SME AI Grant Applications

Very small firms often lose points for avoidable issues:

- Vague use cases with no measurable baseline
- Overstated technical capability without delivery evidence
- Unclear ownership of data governance and compliance
- Missing commercialisation pathway after prototype
- Unrealistic delivery timelines for tiny teams
- Generic “AI trend” language not tied to customer demand

A concise, grounded proposal usually outperforms a highly ambitious but under-evidenced one.

## Managing AI-Specific Risks in the Proposal

Even early-stage projects should include clear controls for:

- **Data quality and access risk**: provenance, completeness, and permissions
- **Model performance risk**: validation approach and acceptance criteria
- **Compliance risk**: sector rules, privacy expectations, auditability
- **Operational risk**: fallback processes if model performance drops
- **Adoption risk**: user training and workflow integration

Risk sections should demonstrate active project management, not simply list concerns.

## 30-Day Application Preparation Plan

### Week 1: Opportunity Qualification

- Shortlist 3–5 relevant calls
- Check eligibility, timelines, and match-funding levels
- Decide “go/no-go” based on strategic fit

### Week 2: Evidence and Scope

- Define baseline metrics and target outcomes
- Finalise technical scope and project boundaries
- Collect supporting evidence (customer demand, pilots, letters)

### Week 3: Delivery and Financial Model

- Build work packages and milestone plan
- Draft budget with cost rationale and dependencies
- Confirm partner roles and resource commitments

### Week 4: Writing and Review

- Write full application against scoring criteria
- Run an internal red-team review for clarity and risk gaps
- Finalise attachments and submit before deadline pressure

## Final Recommendations

For very small UK companies, AI grants can accelerate capability and reduce early-stage innovation risk—but only when the project case is disciplined and evidence-driven. Focus on a narrow, high-value use case, present a realistic plan for delivery, and make impact metrics easy for assessors to score.

If resources are limited, prioritize proposal quality over proposal volume: one strong application to a well-matched call is usually better than multiple weak submissions. Pair practical ambition with transparent budgeting, clear accountability, and measurable outcomes.
